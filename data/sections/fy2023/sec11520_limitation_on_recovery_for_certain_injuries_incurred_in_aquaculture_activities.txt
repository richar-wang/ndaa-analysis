SEC. 11520.  LIMITATION ON RECOVERY FOR CERTAIN INJURIES INCURRED IN AQUACULTURE ACTIVITIES. (a)  In General .— Section 30104 of title 46, United States Code ,  is amended — (1)  by  inserting  “ (a)  In General.— ” before the first sentence; and 
 (2)  by  adding  at the end the following: “(b)  Limitation on Recovery by Aquaculture Workers .— “(1)  In general .— For purposes of subsection (a), the term ‘ seaman ’ does not include an individual who— “(A)  is an aquaculture worker if State workers’ compensation is available to such individual; and 
 “(B)  was, at the time of injury, engaged in aquaculture in a place where such individual had lawful access. 
 
 “(2)  Aquaculture worker defined .— In this subsection, the term ‘ aquaculture worker ’ means an individual who— “(A)  is employed by a commercial enterprise that is involved in the controlled cultivation and harvest of aquatic plants and animals, including— “(i)  the cleaning, processing, or canning of fish and fish products; 
 “(ii)  the cultivation and harvesting of shellfish; and 
 “(iii)  the controlled growing and harvesting of other aquatic species; 
 
 “(B)  does not hold a license issued under section 7101(c); and 
 “(C)  is not required to hold a merchant mariner credential under part F of subtitle II.”

 (b)  46 USC 30104 note . Applicability .— The amendments made by this section shall apply to an injury incurred on or after the date of enactment of this Act.
