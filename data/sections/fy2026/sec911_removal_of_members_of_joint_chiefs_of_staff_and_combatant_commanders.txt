911. Removal of members of Joint Chiefs of Staff and combatant commanders (a) Joint Chiefs of Staff Section 151 of title 10, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection: (h) Removal of members of Joint Chiefs of Staff (1) If the President removes a member of the Joint Chiefs of Staff from office or transfers a member of the Joint Chiefs of Staff to another position or location before the end of the term of the member as specified in statute, the President shall, not later than five days after the removal or transfer takes effect, submit to Congress, including the congressional defense committees, notice that the member is being removed or transferred and a statement of the reason for the removal or transfer.  (2) Nothing in this subsection prohibits a personnel action authorized by another provision of law. . (b) Combatant commanders Section 164(a) of title 10, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph: (3) (A) If the President removes an officer assigned under paragraph (1) to serve as the commander of a unified or specified combatant command or transfers that officer to another position or location before the expected end of the officer's service as a combatant commander, the President shall, not later than five days after the removal or transfer takes effect, submit to Congress, including the congressional defense committees, notice that the officer is being removed or transferred and a statement of the reason for the removal or transfer.  (B) Nothing in this paragraph prohibits a personnel action authorized by another provision of law. .
